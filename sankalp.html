Safe Scavengers Sankalp

A Step Towards Safety, Dignity, and Systemic Change for Sanitation Workers.

The Challenge

Launched in April 2025, the "Safe Scavengers Sankalp" initiative confronts the harsh realities faced by manual scavengers and sanitation workers. These essential members of our society often work in perilous conditions without adequate protection, facing health risks and social stigma.

Our Approach

With a dedicated team of 30 volunteers, we began by deeply engaging with the community. Our process involves listening to their experiences, learning about their daily struggles, and actively creating awareness among all stakeholders about the risks and systemic challenges inherent in this critical work.

What We Found: Insights from the Field

A survey conducted with 40 manual scavengers provided critical insights:

Demographics

Participants ranged from early 20s to over 50, offering a diverse perspective on the issues.

Nature of Work

82.5% involved in general drain cleaning; others clean septic tanks and engage in manual scavenging.

Experience

Significant experience: 32.5% with over 10 years, and another 32.5% with 6-10 years.

Safety Equipment

Basic tools like buckets, shovels, gloves, and masks are used, but essential safety gear like boots and protective clothing is mostly unavailable.

Challenges Faced

Lack of safety gear, low/irregular wages, social stigma, poor working conditions, and no healthcare support are urgent issues.

What They Need

  • Quality safety equipment
  • Regular health checkups
  • Skill development for alternative jobs
  • Legal support and advocacy
  • Better access to government welfare schemes

Our Next Steps

  • Creating impactful awareness campaigns for safety and dignity.
  • Engaging with local authorities and communities for systemic support.
  • Facilitating access to safety gear, health checkups, and welfare schemes.
  • Exploring skill development programs for safer, sustainable livelihoods.

Join Us in This Vital Mission

The issue of sanitation worker safety and dignity requires collective action from citizens, businesses, and policymakers. Your support can help us expand our efforts and bring about meaningful change.
